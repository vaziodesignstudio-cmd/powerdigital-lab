## What Katapult is

A fintech that offers lease-purchase (a buy-now-pay-later alternative) to underserved, nonprime consumers who are overlooked by traditional credit. Proprietary AI and ML predict consumer behavior more accurately than a credit score, so people can buy what they can afford and pay it down on terms that fit their needs. Katapult serves two sides: consumers and the retailers/merchants who integrate it.

Market context used in messaging:
- 64 million Americans underserved by existing credit options.
- 38% of US consumers are nonprime and underserved.
- 40 to 50 billion dollar e-commerce durable goods opportunity for retailers.

## Brand Idea

"Seeing the good in people is good for business."

It gives the brand its emotional dimension and guides look, feel, and voice. Respect, dignity, trust, empowerment, and inclusion are the feelings the brand is built on.

## Brand Positioning

"Katapult is humanizing the way underserved consumers get the things they need with payment solutions based on fairness, transparency, and dignity."

## Brand Pillars

- Why: People Deserve Better. Underserved consumers deserve fairness, transparency, and dignity, and partners who see the whole picture of their financial journey.
- What: Reduce the Friction. Easy for consumers (no hoops to get approval) and easy for retailers (integrates with existing tech).
- How: AI With Dignity. Predicts behavior better than credit scores; gives people freedom without getting in over their heads; shares insights with retailers.

## Emotional Impact (four emotions across the journey)

- Awareness: makes people feel elevated (also: seen, lifted up, honored).
- Involvement: makes people feel respected (also: appreciated, valued, admired).
- Engagement: makes people feel empowered (also: trusted, legitimized, confident).
- Loyalty: makes people feel included (also: embraced, committed, involved).

## Brand Voice

Audiences: employees, shoppers, retailers, investors, media. Three voice modes, with approved examples:

- Down-to-earth and direct: "We can help you get that." / "A personalized plan you can trust that also trusts you." / "We do the hard work to keep things simple." / "The oh-so-simple application."
- Engaging and friendly: "Your path from 'maybe' to 'all mine.'" / "On your mark, set, shop." / "We treat human beings like, well, human beings." / "We make integration a snap."
- Aspirational and positive: "You're more than a credit score." / "We're AI with dignity. Fintech with heart." / "Work for a company where humanity is a required skill." / "It's a little good karma for commerce."

Signature line: "We treat human beings like, well, human beings."

### Writing style

- Headlines engage and entertain. The headline is the invitation to the story, not the whole story.
- Keep sentences short and focused. Keep the language simple. This is what makes the brand approachable and energetic.
- Write like you are talking to a friend. Sound like a real person, not a salesperson. Read sentences aloud to check they feel natural.
- Find a place for something playful, usually in the headline or the start or end of a paragraph. A little humor is a bonus. Do not overdo puns or alliteration.

## Logo

Custom-drawn wordmark based on the Jeko typeface. Conveys a dynamic, in-motion feeling.

- Never recreate, modify, redraw, recolor, or change the font. Always use supplied artwork.
- Color variations: Katapult Pink (primary, use whenever possible), Katapult Dark Blue (secondary), White (on solid-colored backgrounds), 100K Black (print only, special cases such as newspaper or etching).
- Clear space equals the x-height of the logo, kept free of any other elements.
- Minimum size: digital 96 px, print 1 in / 25 mm.
- The logo may sit on any primary palette color with enough contrast, but never on imagery or photography.
- Do not: vertically or horizontally scale, skew, distort, rotate, outline, crop, add effects, place on photography, or use in running text.

### Digital icon and favicon

A "K" combined with the Bounce. Available square and round for app, social, and purchase-flow use. Favicon uses a thicker Bounce stroke. The icon never replaces the logo in marketing communications.

## Typography

- Primary: Oakes Grotesk (grotesque sans serif, minimalist industrial feel). Weights used: Light, Regular, Medium, Bold, plus italics. License: youworkforthem.com.
- Secondary: Teodor (serif), for large quotes and callouts. Regular and Medium, plus italics. License: displaay.net.
- Alternate: Open Sans for non-marketing documents (Office, Google Workspace), free from Google Fonts. Arial as a system fallback for email and signatures.

Usage rules:
- Large headlines: Oakes Grotesk Medium.
- Subheads: Oakes Grotesk Bold. Body: Oakes Grotesk Regular. Bold for emphasis within text. Italics only for titles and names of works.
- Large quotes: Teodor Medium. Attributions: Oakes Grotesk Bold and Regular.

## Photography style

Depicts end consumers (individuals and families) celebrating and connecting around the products Katapult helps them buy.

- Individual portraits: a single subject against a plain, solid-colored background that complements the brand colors.
- People are the primary focal point. Subjects look like real people, not models, across a variety of ages and backgrounds. Authentic, natural, fun.
- Touches of color that reflect or complement the brand palette.

Do not: use images without people; crop out faces; show people annoyed, upset, or moody; use black and white; apply special color treatments; use low-contrast, too-dark, too-light, or blown-out images; use textured or patterned backgrounds for individual portraits. Always show people uplifted by access to the things they need to thrive.

## Iconography style

A visual language of movement, gesture, and connection.

- Drawn with arrows, arcs, and dotted lines to convey continuous movement within simplified shapes.
- One line weight per icon, matched across a set.
- Maximum two colors per icon. On white or Cream: Dark Blue with Pink accent. On Pink: accent becomes white. On Dark Blue: Light Blue with Pink accents.

## Illustration style

Builds on iconography with more shapes and movement in simplified environments. Two colors against a solid background. Katapult Pink is always present, as background, accent, or movement lines. One line weight per illustration.

## Animation style

Motion conveys action, expression, and ease through the Katapult Bounce: an energetic line that moves and leaps frame to frame, bounces off objects, connects words and ideas, and bounces into action to activate the logo. High energy, colorful.

## Graphic language (the Katapult Bounce)

Arcs, angles, and shapes inspired by the logo. The Bounce should be seen, heard, and felt.

- Super Graphic: large arcs at any scale, no copy needed.
- Support Graphic: linear arcs and angles in dotted and solid lines that support copy. The left side of an arc or angle is always dotted to signal progression of movement. Dynamic crops add to the story.
- Making Connections: arcs and angles connect words and ideas and signal progression in infographics.
- Interactions: arcs may traverse over images or behind portraits, placed over neutral areas, never covering faces. Crops can also convey the arc.
