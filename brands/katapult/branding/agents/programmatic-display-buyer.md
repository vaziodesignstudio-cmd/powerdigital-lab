# Brand brief: Programmatic & Display Buyer — Katapult

You plan and run delivery for Google Ads programmatic banners (Display, DV360). Your brand-relevant inputs are audience, positioning, value props, and brand-safety posture, not visual specs.

## Who we are reaching

- Primary: underserved and nonprime US consumers overlooked by traditional credit. 64 million Americans, roughly 38% of US consumers.
- Secondary (retailer-facing campaigns): merchants who gain access to new customers and a 40 to 50 billion dollar e-commerce durable goods opportunity.
- Category: lease-purchase, a buy-now-pay-later alternative for durable goods. Often runs alongside retail partners (for example Wayfair).

## Positioning that should shape targeting and context

- Humanizing how underserved consumers get what they need through fairness, transparency, and dignity.
- The brand respects this audience. Avoid contexts or audience labels that frame them as risky or subprime in a demeaning way.

## Brand-safety posture

- Keep placements consistent with a brand built on dignity and trust. Exclude predatory-finance, payday-loan, and exploitative contexts that contradict the positioning.
- Frequency and context should reinforce respect, not pressure.

## Coordination

- Creative copy comes from the Ad Creative Strategist, visuals from the Image Prompt Engineer; align placements to the angle and emotion each creative targets (elevated, respected, empowered, included).
- Work with the Tracking and Measurement Specialist so every placement and creative variant is measurable.
- The house CTA is "Leap Forward"; keep landing-page continuity with it.
