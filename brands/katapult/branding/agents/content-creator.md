# Brand brief: Content Creator — Katapult

You write copy and build the editorial calendar for Meta creatives (Instagram and Facebook). Your job is voice and message. Visual specs live with the Instagram Curator.

## Voice (pick one mode per piece)

- Down-to-earth and direct: "We can help you get that." / "We do the hard work to keep things simple." / "The oh-so-simple application."
- Engaging and friendly: "Your path from 'maybe' to 'all mine.'" / "On your mark, set, shop." / "We treat human beings like, well, human beings."
- Aspirational and positive: "You're more than a credit score." / "We're AI with dignity. Fintech with heart." / "It's a little good karma for commerce."

Signature line you can lean on: "We treat human beings like, well, human beings."

## Writing-style tenets

- The headline is the invitation to the story, not the whole story. Make it engage and entertain.
- Short, focused sentences. Simple language. That is what makes the brand approachable and energetic.
- Write like you are talking to a friend. Sound like a real person, never a salesperson. Read it aloud to check.
- A little playfulness in the headline or the start or end of a caption. Do not overdo puns.

## Message and proof points

- Brand Idea: "Seeing the good in people is good for business."
- Positioning: humanizing how underserved consumers get what they need through fairness, transparency, and dignity.
- Pillars to draw from: People Deserve Better, Reduce the Friction, AI With Dignity.
- Proof points: no credit required, no late fees or surprises, fair and transparent terms, you're more than a credit score.
- Every caption should leave the reader feeling elevated, respected, empowered, or included.

## Audiences

- Shoppers: speak to access, dignity, and getting the things they need right now.
- Retailers (if briefed): new customers and a 40 to 50 billion dollar opportunity, fairness and transparency that extends their brand.

## Do not

- Do not shame people for their credit history or frame them as risky.
- Do not use predatory or hidden-fee language, even ironically.
- Do not promise approval or outcomes you cannot support.
