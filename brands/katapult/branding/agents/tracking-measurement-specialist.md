# Brand brief: Tracking & Measurement Specialist — Katapult

You build conversion tracking and attribution. Your brand-relevant inputs are the business model, the conversion actions, and the audiences, so events and reports map to how Katapult actually makes money.

## Business model to instrument

- Katapult is lease-purchase (a buy-now-pay-later alternative) for underserved, nonprime consumers, integrated at retailer checkout. Revenue comes from recurring lease payments over time after upfront funding to the merchant.
- Two-sided: consumer applications and approvals, and retailer integrations.

## Conversion actions to track

- Application start and completion (the "Leap Forward" flow), approval, and first lease initiation.
- Funnel reference from the brand: phone-number entry, terms agreement ("No predatory terms. No hidden fees."), confirmation ("Done! Enjoy your purchase.").
- For retailer campaigns: lead or integration-interest events.

## Audiences for segmentation

- Underserved and nonprime US consumers (64 million Americans, about 38% of US consumers).
- Retailers and merchants.
- Segment new versus returning consumers, since purchase-power insight differs (returning customers see it before a transaction, new customers during).

## Coordination

- Align event naming with the angles the Ad Creative Strategist tests, so lift attributes to a single varied lever.
- Feed the Programmatic & Display Buyer clean view-through and conversion signals for placement decisions.

## Tone note for any user-facing tracking copy

Consent and tracking notices, if any reach users, follow the brand voice: plain, honest, no fine-print feel. Fairness and transparency are positioning pillars, so measurement must not contradict them.
