## Formats

- 1:1 square, 1080 x 1080.
- 9:16 vertical, 1080 x 1920.
Both share one visual system; the only real difference is logo placement and vertical breathing room.

## Layout zones (top to bottom)

1. Background: flat light gray, aligned to the brand's Lightest Gray (#f4f4f4). No gradients.
2. Logo: Katapult wordmark in Pink (#ed5370).
   - 9:16: centered at the top.
   - 1:1: bottom-right corner; the top is reserved for the headline.
3. Headline: two lines, Dark Blue (#131340), heavy grotesque in the spirit of Oakes Grotesk Medium/Bold. Reference copy: "No Credit? No Problem."
4. Subheadline / value prop: one line, Pink (#ed5370), uppercase, letter-spaced. Reference copy: "LEASE IT NOW, OWN IT LATER."
5. Hero image: a hand holding a smartphone showing the Katapult app with a grid of retailer logos (Best Buy, Walmart, Wayfair, Amazon, Target, Home Depot). Real hand, natural skin, product as the focal point. Sits center-to-lower in both formats.
6. Legal disclaimer: small gray text, centered (9:16) or bottom-left (1:1).

## Disclaimer copy (reuse verbatim, adjust state list per legal)

> This is an advertisement for a lease-purchase or lease-to-own service. The path to ownership will cost more than the merchant's cash price. Certain items available at participating locations. Not available in MN, NJ, WI, WY.

## How this maps to the brand

- Colors are on-system: Lightest Gray background, Dark Blue headline, Pink subhead and logo.
- Headline follows the voice (short, direct, a little playful: "No Credit? No Problem.").
- Photography rule holds: real hand, product-forward, well lit, no filters, logo never sits on the photo.
- The Bounce (arcs) is not used in this particular template; keep that in mind if a brief asks to add graphic language, and clear it with the Brand Guardian first.

## For the agents (when the banner sequence is briefed)

- Image Prompt Engineer: reproduce the zone structure and the hand-holding-phone hero; use the exact hex values; exclude filters, black and white, cropped hands.
- Ad Creative Strategist: write headline + uppercase subhead pairs in this rhythm; default CTA stays "Leap Forward" unless a brief says otherwise; always carry the legal disclaimer.
- Instagram Curator: the 1:1 and 9:16 here double as the Meta creative frames.
